THE SCIENCE
🧬 What is Turing Morphogenesis?

In 1952, mathematician Alan Turing published a paper called "The Chemical Basis of Morphogenesis" — showing mathematically that two simple chemicals, reacting and diffusing through tissue, can spontaneously produce complex patterns like spots and stripes. No blueprint required. The pattern emerges on its own.

🟡 BMP — the Activator

BMP (Bone Morphogenetic Protein) is the gold particle. It does two things: it excites itself (nearby BMP attracts more BMP) and it triggers the production of Noggin. Think of it as a signal that says "pattern here."

Short range → attracts other BMP → clusters form
Long range → repels other BMP → clusters space apart
🔵 Noggin — the Inhibitor

Noggin is the cyan particle. It diffuses faster than BMP (lighter, moves quicker) and suppresses BMP wherever it reaches. It fills the gaps between BMP clusters, preventing new spots from forming there.

Always repels other Noggin → spreads into gaps
Slowly drifts toward BMP → contains each blob
⚡ The Turing Instability

The magic happens because of a speed difference. Noggin diffuses faster than BMP. So BMP can locally excite itself before Noggin arrives to suppress it — but Noggin then spreads out and suppresses BMP everywhere else. The result: stable evenly-spaced spots, with suppressed gaps between them.

This is called a Turing Instability — a uniform distribution is unstable, and tiny random fluctuations amplify into a stable pattern.

🗺 The environment zones
BMP Organiser (amber ring, top-right)
Mimics the Zone of Polarising Activity in a developing limb bud. BMP inside gets pulled toward the centre — a region of strong autocatalytic signal that seeds patterning.
Noggin Suppressor (teal ring, bottom-left)
Mimics a Noggin-rich tissue domain (e.g. dorsal ectoderm). All bodies inside slow down — high Noggin concentration suppresses BMP diffusion and dampens pattern formation.

🔁 Spot vs Stripe mode (press T)

In spot mode, BMP attracts itself at short range → discrete blobs. In stripe mode, BMP repels at all distances → filaments. Turing proved both emerge from the same equations — only the diffusion ratio changes. Real animals are "locked in" at birth, but you can switch live here.
